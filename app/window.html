Drag and Drop Your Oculus Rift Configuration

To use your calibration and profile data, drag and drop your Devices.json and Profiles.json files into the region below. These files are located in the following directories, depending on your operating system:

  • Windows: C:\User\<username>\AppData\Local\Oculus
  • OSX: /Library/Preferences/Oculus
  • Linux: /home/<username>/.config/Oculus

Getting started with Cupola VR Viewer

  • Enter the URL for a Cupola-supported virtual environment into the URL field and press Enter.
  • Make sure your Oculus Rift is connected and press Connect to Rift. Cupola VR Viewer will start to receive data directly from the Rift hardware and send it to the virtual environment.
  • You can make Cupola VR Viewer go in and out of fullscreen by pressing the Toggle Fullscreen button.

Example environments:

Rings -- a simple virtual environment with no motion controls, only head-tracking from the Rift.

In the simulation, press "toggle render mode" to switch to the Rift camera mode. In Cupola, click Connect to Rift to start using your Rift with the simulation.

Oculus Bridge First-Person -- a Cupola-supported version of Instrument's "First Person" demo for oculus-bridge, to show how simple it is to connect Cupola with existing code.

In the simulation, press "toggle render mode" to switch to the Rift camera mode. In Cupola, click Connect to Rift to start using your Rift with the simulation.

While in Rift mode, use the WSAD keys to move around. Check the "help" button inside the simulation for additional control information.

Cupola VR Viewer Configuration

  • To use your Oculus configuration data and send device/profile-specific data to the simulation, check out the Config section.
  • If the orientation data coming in from the Rift feels like it's consistently behind, try adjusting the Predict time slider in the Config section.
